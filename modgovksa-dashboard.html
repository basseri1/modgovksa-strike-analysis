Key Observations

14x Escalation in 2 Weeks

Attack tempo surged from ~5 drones/day (Mar 6) to 70 drones/day (Mar 20), showing a dramatic and sustained escalation.

US Embassy Struck (Mar 3)

Two drones struck the US Embassy in Riyadh, causing fire and minor material damage — confirmed direct hit before interception campaign began.

Oil Infrastructure Focus (Week 1)

Shaybah oilfield was the most targeted asset during Mar 6-12, with multiple daily waves of drones through the Empty Quarter.

Shift to Urban Targets (Week 2+)

From mid-March, Eastern Province and Riyadh became primary targets, replacing oil infrastructure as the main focus.

Missiles Evade Defenses

At least 2 ballistic missiles fell in uninhabited areas (Mar 7 and Mar 22) — not intercepted but missed populated zones.

Debris Near Critical Sites

Interception debris fell near Prince Sultan Air Base and a Riyadh refinery (Mar 18), and caused a limited fire in Riyadh (Mar 2).

Prince Sultan Air Base Targeted

Ballistic missiles specifically aimed at the base hosting US military — showing intent to strike American military assets on Saudi soil.

Purely Factual Tone

@modgovksa never attributes attacks to any party. Posts follow a strict format: "Interception and destruction of [X] in [location]."
